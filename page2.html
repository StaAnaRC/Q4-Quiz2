Don Santiago De Los Santos (Captain Tiago)
  • Captain Tiago is a short, plump, and young-looking guy with the features of a Filipino.
  • He is good friends with the Ibarras and even hosted a grand party for the return of Crisostomo Ibarra.
  • He became the wealthiest man in Binondo from running an Opium (cigarette) business and owning various lands in Laguna, Pampanga, and San Diego.
  • He, the father of Maria Clara and the husband of Pia Alba, is considered religious in San Diego, as he owns various images of saints and makes generous donations to the church.

Source of Image
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra Y Magsalin (Ibarra)
  • Crisostomo Ibarra is a persevering, intelligent, and respected young man with the features of an ordinary Filipino: brown skin and blushy cheeks.
  • He is Don Rafael Ibarra's son and Maria Clara's lover who just returned to the Philippines after completing his 7-year-long education in Europe.
  • He recognized Europe as his second mother home and the Philippines as his first. His love for his country is evident in how he studied abroad to help his country and fellow Filipinos.

Source of Image
Father Damaso
  • Father Damaso was the former curate of San Diego before he got assigned to a different town, from digging Don Rafael's corpse and moving it to the Chinese graveyard.
  • He is a corrupt Franciscan friar who uses violence and abuse to punish those who have wronged or rebelled against him, including innocent students who were inefficient due to the system of education at the time.
  • He has a superiority complex, as he believes friars are far more powerful than government officials.
  • He always puts himself first and is greedy for money and power. Both components, including the ignorance of the Filipinos, helped him and the other friars reign over them easily.
Rizal, J., & Almario, V. S. (2014). Noli Me Tangere. Adarna House.
